Introduction

About the Graduate Institute of Journalism, National Taiwan University

Cultivating advanced journalism techniques and knowledge through both theory and professional practice.
Developing independent thinking, multicultural awareness, and civic responsibility essential for responsible journalism.
Enhancing an international perspective through academic exchange, cross-campus collaboration, and media research.
Strengthening academic depth by drawing on NTU's rich resources across science, medicine, law, and business.

The Graduate Institute of Journalism at National Taiwan University was established in 1991 to cultivate advanced journalism talent and elevate professional standards in Taiwan's media landscape. Inspired by the world's leading journalism schools yet deeply rooted in Taiwanese society, the institute has grown from admitting just 12 students out of over 600 applicants in its inaugural year into one of NTU's most competitive graduate programs.

The program integrates three streams of resources: working journalists from newspapers, magazines, television, and digital media; cross-campus electives from partner institutions; and the breadth of NTU's academic departments spanning science, engineering, medicine, law, and business. This convergence underpins the institute's signature "learning by doing" philosophy, producing graduates equipped to lead Taiwan's media industry and contribute to global journalism scholarship.

Students may pursue the general journalism track or enroll in the Digital Journalism Specialization Program, with training in data-driven storytelling, social media analysis, and web production. The institute also offers a Bachelor of Social Science pathway for outstanding undergraduates.

Curriculum

Academic programs and course offerings at the Graduate Institute of Journalism

The Graduate Institute of Journalism offers a flexible two-year master's program designed to develop both theoretical rigor and professional skills. Students may choose to specialize in digital journalism, media studies, or pursue an interdisciplinary track drawing from NTU's broader academic community.

The curriculum is structured around three pillars: Core Journalism Courses, which build foundational reporting, writing, and editing skills; Research Methods, which equip students with quantitative and qualitative tools for academic inquiry; and Elective Seminars, which allow students to pursue topics ranging from political communication and media law to data journalism and cross-media storytelling.

The Digital Journalism Specialization Program offers additional training in web production, social media analysis, data visualization, and computational methods. Students in this track complete a capstone project combining journalistic craft with technical innovation.

General Journalism Track

The core graduate journalism program covering reporting, editing, media theory, and research methods. Open to all incoming students.

Digital Journalism Specialization

An advanced track integrating data journalism, web production, and computational analysis. Students complete a digital capstone project.

Bachelor of Social Science Pathway

A special track for outstanding NTU undergraduates combining journalism training with social science research coursework.

Cross-Campus Electives

Students may draw on the full breadth of NTU's departments — science, medicine, law, and business — to deepen subject expertise.
